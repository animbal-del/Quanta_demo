COMPANY OVERVIEW

AgriPulse provides affordable soil sensor networks and AI-driven irrigation
recommendations to smallholder farmers in Maharashtra, India.

The core product is a solar-powered, LoRaWAN-connected soil moisture and
temperature sensor that costs ₹400 to manufacture and deploys in 15 minutes
without any technical expertise. Paired with a WhatsApp-integrated chatbot
(available in Marathi, Hindi, and English), farmers receive daily personalized
irrigation recommendations based on real soil data, weather forecasts, and
crop calendars.

PROBLEM

India's smallholder farmers (holdings < 5 acres) represent 86% of all farm
households but use irrigation entirely based on experience and guesswork.

Consequences:
→ 35-40% excess water usage (ICAR data, 2024)
→ 15-20% annual crop loss attributable to irrigation errors
→ Groundwater depletion in key agricultural districts

Existing precision agriculture solutions (Fasal, Plantix, CropIn) target
medium-to-large farms and cost ₹5,000-₹25,000 per acre annually — completely
out of reach for 2-acre smallholders.

SOLUTION

AgriPulse = ₹400 soil sensor + WhatsApp chatbot + farm management dashboard

Sensor specs:
→ Measures: soil moisture, temperature, electrical conductivity
→ Battery life: 18 months on solar
→ Range: 2km LoRaWAN connectivity
→ Setup time: 15 minutes, no technician needed

Farmer experience:
→ Receives daily WhatsApp message: "Irrigate 45 minutes today, skip tomorrow"
→ Available in Marathi by default
→ Works on basic smartphones (2G compatible)
→ ₹500/month subscription (less than 1% of farm revenue for 2-acre farm)
