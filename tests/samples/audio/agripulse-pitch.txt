— START RECORDING, THEN READ: —

Okay so I met this founder Rahul at an agriculture meetup in Pune last week.
He's building something called AgriPulse — and basically, small farmers in
Maharashtra have no idea when to irrigate their crops. They just guess based
on how the soil looks, and they end up wasting 35 to 40 percent more water
than they need to, and they lose crops worth lakhs every year.

So what Rahul built is this really cheap soil sensor — it costs about 400
rupees to make — and it connects to a simple WhatsApp bot that tells farmers
in Marathi exactly when to water and how much. No app to download, no
technical knowledge needed. Just WhatsApp.

The traction is what got me excited. He has 247 farms on the platform already.
They're paying 500 rupees a month each. And he's seeing 38 percent water
savings on average across those farms.

The founders are interesting — Rahul grew up on a farm in Nashik, his family
actually lost crops to bad irrigation which is why he started this. His
co-founder Kiran is ex-Bosch IoT engineer who built the sensor hardware.

They've also signed an MOU with the Nashik District Agriculture Office to
promote the product to PM-KISAN beneficiary farms. That's meaningful government
validation.

They're raising about 1.5 crore to scale sensor manufacturing and expand to
more districts. I think this is very worth a look.
